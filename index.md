<section id="about" class="tb-section">
  <h2 class="tb-section__title">About TerraBytes</h2>
  <p>
    Earth observation (EO) presents unique challenges and opportunities that set it apart from
    other fields of machine learning and computer vision. EO data is abundant, repeatedly covering
    a large but bounded environment &mdash; our planet &mdash; and the co-location and evolution
    of these observations is a rich, multimodal, multitemporal source of information. Yet the
    distribution of EO data is non-stationary and spatially biased, with large parts of the world
    strongly under-represented.
  </p>
  <p>
    <strong>TerraBytes</strong> is an initiative to address these challenges at the intersection
    of <em>data curation</em>, <em>data archiving</em>, and <em>representation learning</em>,
    fostering a holistic discussion that covers every step from downlinked satellite data to
    training paradigms and downstream applications.
  </p>
  <p>
    After a successful first edition at ICML 2025, TerraBytes returns bigger at
    <strong>ECCV 2026</strong>. Speakers and program details will be shared on this
    page as they are confirmed.
  </p>
</section>

  <h2 class="tb-section__title">Call for papers</h2>

  <div class="tb-cfp__deadline">
    <div class="tb-cfp__deadline-row">
      <div>
        <div class="tb-cfp__deadline-label">Submission deadline</div>
        <div class="tb-cfp__deadline-date">18 June 2026</div>
        <div class="tb-cfp__deadline-tz">23:59 Anywhere on Earth</div>
      </div>
      <div class="tb-cfp__deadline-meta">
        <div><strong>Format</strong> &middot; ECCV 2026 LaTeX style</div>
        <div><strong>Short papers</strong> &middot; 8 pages (excl. references)</div>
        <div><strong>Full papers</strong> &middot; 14 pages (excl. references)</div>
        <div><strong>Submissions</strong> &middot; <a href="https://openreview.net/group?id=thecvf.com%252FECCV%252F2026%252FWorkshop%252FTerraBytes_II">OpenReview</a> </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p>
    We invite submissions to the second edition of TerraBytes, held in conjunction with
    <strong>ECCV 2026</strong> in Malmö, Sweden. TerraBytes brings the Earth observation,
    machine learning, and computer vision communities together to discuss global datasets
    and models for our planet &mdash; covering every step from downlinked satellite data
    to training paradigms and downstream applications.
  </p>

  <h3 class="tb-cfp__heading">Topics of interest</h3>
  <p>We welcome submissions on (but not limited to):</p>
  <ul class="tb-cfp__topics">
    <li>
      <strong>Large-scale Earth observation datasets and benchmarks</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>curation of new large-scale vision datasets for EO,</li>
        <li>augmentation techniques for EO data and their impact on learning, data traceability, and expansion of existing datasets,</li>
        <li>re-use and adoption of existing datasets in new scenarios,</li>
        <li>impact of training data quality and size on downstream tasks under domain shifts.</li>
      </ul>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Efficient and continual representation learning for remote sensing</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>weakly- and self-supervised learning for EO data,</li>
        <li>domain adaptation strategies to deal with temporal, geographical, or sensor gaps,</li>
        <li>continual and online learning, to update models when facing distribution shifts,</li>
        <li>active learning and reinforcement learning.</li>
      </ul>
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Real-world applications</strong>
      <ul>
        <li>early detection, monitoring and assessment of disasters (floods, landslides, wildfires),</li>
        <li>large-scale Land Cover and Land Use mapping,</li>
        <li>2D and 3D change detection in urban and rural areas,</li>
        <li>estimation of biophysical parameters (biomass, biodiversity, soil stress, etc.).</li>
      </ul>
    </li>
  </ul>

  <h3 class="tb-cfp__heading">Submission process</h3>
  <p>
    Submission to the TerraBytes workshop is <strong>double-blind</strong>. We accept
    <strong>short papers</strong> (8 pages excluding references) and
    <strong>full-length papers</strong> (14 pages excluding references). Authors must
    follow the standard ECCV 2026 paper format (LaTeX style files will be linked here
    once released by the conference).
  </p>
  <p>
    Short papers can describe work in progress, opinion pieces, or datasets and research
    papers that have been published in another venue (conference or journal) within the
    last 6 months on topics relevant to TerraBytes. For already-published work, please
    submit a summarised short paper &mdash; <strong><em>journal papers</em> are
    particularly welcome, as the workshop is a great venue to bring them to a wider
    audience.</strong>
  </p>
  <p>
    All submissions go through <strong><a href="https://openreview.net/group?id=thecvf.com%252FECCV%252F2026%252FWorkshop%252FTerraBytes_II">OpenReview</a></strong>.
    Each paper is reviewed by at least two non-conflicting reviewers. All accepted papers
    are presented in a dedicated poster session; full-length papers and selected short
    papers will also be given spotlight presentations throughout the day.
  </p>
  <p>
    It is expected that at least one author of each accepted paper will register for the
    workshop and present the paper in person. Online presentations will be considered for
    presenters who cannot travel (e.g. visa or personal constraints).
  </p>

  <h3 class="tb-cfp__heading">Important dates</h3>
  <p><em>All deadlines are <strong>23:59 Anywhere on Earth</strong>.</em></p>
  <ul class="tb-cfp__dates">
    <li><strong>Paper submission deadline:</strong> 18 June 2026</li>

  </ul>

  <h3 class="tb-cfp__heading">Proceedings</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Accepted full-length papers can be published in an archival proceedings
    volume</strong> (opt-in). Authors who prefer their paper to remain non-archival
    &mdash; for example to preserve future submission options &mdash; may choose that
    route instead. Exact details (proceedings venue, formatting requirements) are being
    finalised and will be announced here.
  </p>
